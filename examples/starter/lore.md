# Sample Lore — The Waystation

This document is a minimal example of what a `lore.md` can look like. The
bootstrap script reads the whole file verbatim, so you can write it in any
prose style. Sections, tone, proper nouns, and explicit constraints all carry
through to the generator.

## Setting

A small Waystation on a trade road that goes nowhere important anymore. The
town it served dried up eight winters ago when the copper ran out, but the
road still runs through, and travellers still stop here because there is
nowhere else for a day's walk in either direction.

The Waystation itself is a single stone building with a stable, a well, and
a stone bench outside for waiting. A woman named Ibo keeps it — she was not
born here but has been here longer than the copper has been gone. She keeps
a ledger of everyone who passes through. She does not explain why.

## Tone

Dry, weather-worn. People here do not talk about what they have lost
because everyone has lost something. Kindness exists but is transactional
by default — you do a favour, you expect one back, not today but someday.
Humour is the kind that appears in long silences. Nothing is magical.

## Constraints

- The story_arc should be about the Waystation being passed through, not
  being saved. Whatever tension exists should remain unresolved.
- Ibo is a named character. Include her.
- At least one traveller passing through should be in the seed.
- Copper mining references are fine but the mines are dead.

## What is NOT in the setting

- No fantasy races. Everyone is a person.
- No magic. No supernatural elements.
- No heroes. No villains. No quests.
